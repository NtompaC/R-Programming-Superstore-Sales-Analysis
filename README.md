# Project: Superstore Sales & Profitability Analysis

**R Programming Portfolio Project**  
**Author:** Mutono Ntompa  
**Location:** Nairobi, Kenya

## Business Problem
Sales and profitability analysis for a leading Kenyan supermarket chain (inspired by Naivas, QuickMart, or Carrefour). The goal was to identify key drivers of sales, profit margins, and the impact of discounts to support better business decisions.

## Dataset
Superstore Sales Dataset (~9,994 orders) from Kaggle, adapted to a Kenyan retail context.

## Skills Demonstrated
- **R Programming**: `tidyverse`, `lubridate`, `dplyr`
- **Data Cleaning & Transformation**
- **Exploratory Data Analysis**
- **Statistical Analysis** (linear regression on profit drivers)
- **Advanced Visualization**: `ggplot2`

## Key Insights
- Overall profit margin: ~12-15%
- Technology category shows the highest profit margins
- High discount rates significantly reduce profit
- Steady sales growth observed over the years
- Regional performance varies — strong results in Central and South regions

## Project Structure
- `0_Raw_Dataset/` — Original CSV
- `2_Data_Model/` — Data cleaning and analysis scripts
- `3_Visualizations/` — ggplot2 script and interactive report

## How to Reproduce
1. Open the project in RStudio (`Superstore_Sales_Analysis.Rproj`)
2. Run `01_data_import_and_cleaning.R`
3. Run `02_analysis_and_modeling.R`

## Recommendations for Kenyan Retail Context
- Focus expansion on high-margin categories (Technology & Office Supplies)
- Review discount strategy — deep discounts are hurting profitability
- Use regional insights to replicate success across stores
- Consider customer segmentation by purchase behavior
